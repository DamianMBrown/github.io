Way of Kings Part Summaries


Prologue

In the prologue of "The Way of Kings," Szeth-son-son-Vallano, known as Truthless of Shinovar, is compelled by the Parshendi to assassinate King Gavilar on the very day a treaty is being signed between the Kingdom of Alethkar and the Parshendi. Despite his confusion over the betrayal of the newly formed treaty, Szeth obediently carries out his orders, utilizing his rare abilities as a Surgebinder and wielding an Honorblade. In a violent confrontation, he confronts Gavilar, who is revealed to be the king himself, and ultimately slays him. Before dying, Gavilar entrusts Szeth with a mysterious glowing orb and cryptically urges him to convey a vital message to his brother. Szeth, bound by tradition, fulfills Gavilar's last wish before vanishing into the night.




Part One: Above Silence

Cenn, a new recruit, joins Kaladin's squad during a battle where chaos ensues. Kaladin's squad proves to be organized and efficient amidst the turmoil. However, Cenn gets separated from the group and is saved by Kaladin just in time. Meanwhile, Kaladin, branded as a dangerous slave, struggles to maintain his sanity in a slave caravan. He encounters a mysterious windspren and witnesses the harsh treatment of fellow slaves by their captors.
Shallan arrives in Kharbranth, hoping to become the ward of Jasnah Kholin, a renowned scholar. She impresses Jasnah with her writing and logical arguments but faces rejection due to her lack of education in history and philosophy. Despite this setback, Shallan remains determined to save her family's fortunes by any means necessary.
Kaladin's journey as a slave leads him to the warcamp of Highprince Sadeas at the Shattered Plains. Disappointed by the disorderly state of the army, he nonetheless finds purpose in the prospect of fighting again. However, his hopes are dashed when he and his fellow slaves are assigned to the dreaded bridge crews. Despite facing adversity, Kaladin begins to assert himself among the bridgemen, determined to protect them at all costs.



Part Two: The Illuminating Storms

Kaladin takes charge of Bridge Four amidst the trials of Sadeas's warcamp, urging them to push through challenges while confronting prejudice and cruelty. As tensions rise in Hearthstone with the arrival of Brightlord Roshone, Kaladin's beliefs about lighteyes are tested.
Shallan secures her place as Jasnah's ward, driven by the need to salvage her family's fortunes. In Kharbranth, she delves into her studies, navigating the intricacies of politics and society while gaining invaluable insights.
Witnessing his father's bravery during a chasmfiend attack, Adolin grapples with the escalating tensions among the highprinces and doubts about Dalinar's actions. Despite the turmoil, he remains steadfast in his loyalty to his family, confronting the aftermath of the attack amidst rising suspicions and political intrigue.
Meanwhile, Dalinar navigates the complexities of political maneuvering and personal struggles, facing rumors about his condition and tension with Lady Navani at the King's Feast. As Sadeas's rise to Highprince of Information adds to the turmoil, Dalinar must navigate alliances and confront doubts about his leadership on the Shattered Plains.

With Jasnah's help, Shallan studies hard but also struggles with her own thoughts. She tries to steal the Soulcaster but feels too guilty to go through with it. She discusses religion with Kabsal and faces her fears. Then, she discovers strange creatures in her drawings and visits a weird place called Shadesmar. Things get worse when Kabsal tries to poison Shallan, but Jasnah saves her. Shallan feels bad about what happened and worries about her future. Kaladin leads Bridge Four in training exercises, but Gaz and Lamaril discuss his potential threat. Despite this, Kaladin continues to train his men, even introducing a new technique. During a highstorm, Kaladin is left tied up outside as punishment, but miraculously survives. Teft discovers Kaladin's ability to heal with Stormlight, which he keeps secret. Kaladin struggles with his purpose as a bridgeman but eventually decides to train his men as spearmen for a chance to escape. Flashbacks reveal Kaladin's past, including his decision to join the army to protect his brother Tien and his encounter with a Shardbearer during battle. After rejecting the offer of Shardblades, Kaladin is branded a slave by Amaram after witnessing the slaughter of his surviving men.
